singular value decomposition

The SVD of any \(m \times n\) matrix \(A\) expresses it as a matrix product \(A = U\Sigma V^T\), where

  • \(U\) is a \(m \times m\) orthogonal matrix containing the left singular vectors of \(A\)
  • \(V\) is a \(n \times n\) orthogonal matrix containing the right singular vectors of \(A\)
  • \(\Sigma\) is a \(m \times n\) "diagonal" matrix containing the singular values of \(A\)
