vector projections

How do we compare how similar two vectors \(\textbf{a}\) and \(\color{blue}{\textbf{b}}\) are? We can't just compare the length of the vectors, because vectors have both a magnitude and a direction. One tool that we can use to do this is take the projection of one vector onto another, in other words, the component of \(\color{blue}{\textbf{b}}\) that lies along the direction of \(\textbf{a}\). We denote this projection vector as \(\color{red}{\text{proj}_a(b)}\). Multiplying the magnitudes of \(\color{red}{\text{proj}_a(b)}\) and \(\textbf{a}\) gives us the dot product similarity between the two original vectors, which you may be familiar with.

\(| \color{red}{\text{proj}_a(b)} \color{black}||\textbf{a}| = |\textbf{a}|| \color{blue}{\textbf{b}} \color{black}|\cos(\theta) = \textbf{a}\cdot\color{blue}{\textbf{b}}\)
